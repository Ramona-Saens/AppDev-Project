Space Technology and Satellite communication

Space and satellite communication encompasses using artificial satellites orbiting Earth to relay signals for various telecommunications applications like TV, radio, internet, and data, enabling global connectivity and bridging geographical gaps. Space communication  is the exchange of data that astronauts onboard space stations and on space missions carry out with their Earth-bound mission centres.

SATELLITE-BASED INTERNET

Satellite internet is internet access delivered through a wireless connection created by signals transmitted between a satellite and an antenna (or dish) on a home or business.

  • SATELLITE-BASED INTERNET
    EXAMPLES

    1. Starlink is a high-speed satellite internet company, first announced in 2015, Starlink is a constellation of satellites by SpaceX intended to provide a high-speed and low-latency connection to the internet anywhere in the world. Starlink satellites on the other hand are in low earth orbit at an altitude of about 550 km, it relies on thousands of satellites moving overhead so that as some move out of range, more come into range.

    2. Viasat is a provider of high-speed satellite broadband services and secure networking systems covering military and commercial markets. The satellite has a geostationary orbit over North America at 115.1° West longitude. Initially the satellite broadband internet was launched as a response to the lack of coverage provided by terrestrial services.

    3. Hughesnet is a Satellite Internet Provider (satellite ISP). Hughesnet is powered by a state-of-the-art satellite that’s engineered to deliver a faster, better internet experience even in areas with slow or no high-speed options.

    4.The Globalstar system is designed to produce high quality satellite-based telephony services to a broad range of users. Globalstar has a constellation of 48 Low-Earth Orbiting (LEO) satellites that pick up signals from over 80% of the Earth’s surface.

    PURPOSE OF SATELLITE-BASED INTERNET

    Provide internet access to remote and underserved areas where traditional infrastructure like fiber optic cables cannot reach, effectively bridging the digital divide by enabling connectivity in rural locations, during emergencies, and for applications like maritime and aviation where terrestrial options are limited; essentially offering global internet access regardless of geographical location.

  • GPS ADVANCEMENT
    What is it?
    Global Positioning System is a worldwide satellite navigation system that synchronizes time, location, and velocity. Its history begins with the Soviet Union's launch of the Sputnik 1 satellite during the Cold War, which marked the start of the space race. GPS works by utilizing a network of at least 24 satellites in orbit around the planet, and each of which sends out signal with the current time and position.
    Segments of GPS:
    Space (Satellites) these are satelites circling the earth, transmitting signals to users on geographical position and time of day Ground Control the control segment is made uo of earth-based monitor stations, master control stations and ground antenna. Iy includes tracking and operating the satellites in space and monitoring transmissions. User equipment GPS receivers and transmitters include items like smartphones, watches, and telematic devices.

    Five (5) main uses of GPS:

    Location - for determining a position.

    Navigation - for getting from one location to another.

    Tracking - for monitoring object or personal movement

    Mapping - for creating maps of the world.

    Timing - for making it possible to take precise time measurements.

  • SPACE WEATHER TRACKING

    The term "space weather" describes the constantly shifting conditions in space, which are mostly caused by the Sun's activity and can have an effect on Earth's infrastructure and technological systems. It includes things like geomagnetic storms, solar flares, and coronal mass ejections (CMEs).

    Benefits of space weather tracking:

    Protecting critical infrastructure: Early warnings allow power companies to take steps to mitigate potential damage to power grids from geomagnetic storms.

    Safeguarding satellite operations: Operators can take preventative measures to protect satellites from disruptions caused by intense solar radiation.

    Improving communication reliability: Advance notice of space weather events can help manage potential disruptions in communication systems.

    Supporting human spaceflight: Astronauts can be alerted to potential hazards during spacewalks or other extravehicular activities.

    Navigation system resilience: GPS systems can be adjusted to minimize disruptions from space weather events.

    Hubble Space Telescope

    This technology avoids atmospheric distortion, allowing it to bring thousands of jaw-dropping images back to Earth. It has helped us determine the age of the universe.

    Tactical Satellite – or TacSat

    A small intelligence, surveillance and reconnaissance spacecraft that will host the first 5G.MIL payload on orbit.

    Nuclear Space Propulsion and Power

    It offer increased efficiency, reduced fuel consumption and longer mission durations, enabling spacecraft to maneuver between Earth orbits and expand interplanetary travel.

    Joint Emergent Technology Supplying On-orbit Nuclear Power (JETSON) Project

    JETSON is part of the Space Technology Advanced Research - Fast-tracking Innovative Software and Hardware (STAR-FISH) initiative, which seeks to develop enabling technologies for space-based radioisotope power systems.
